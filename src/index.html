Introduction

The Owl House is an animated series created by Dana Terrace. The show originally aired on Disney Channel on January 10, 2020 and the final episode aired on April 8, 2023. Now, it can be streamed on Disney+, Apple TV and Paramount+. The Owl House focuses on the main character Luz Noceda , as she discovers a magical world called “The Boiling Isles”. She meets a rogue witch named Eda Clawthorne and her companion, King. Despite being a human with no magical powers, Luz becomes Eda’s witch apprentice. The Owl House follows this trio’s crazy adventures in this magical world, meeting new friends along the way, as well as facing enemies.

The Owl House has 3 seasons with 43 episodes in total. This animated series deals with themes of friendship, one's identity, love, adventure, a sense of belonging just to name a few. With its mystical world building and a diverse cast of relatable characters, The Owl House is a series that has many light-hearted and funny moments, but it can also tackle more challenging topics, such as feeling like an outcast, anxiety, and generational trauma.

The animated series received a Peabody award in 2021:

For building a wildly inventive other world that makes room for everyone and giving queer kids a welcome template alongside which to explore their own budding creative energies
- Peabody Awards

About the Creator, Dana Terrace

Dana Terrace previously worked on other popular Disney animated series. She was as a storyboard artist for Gravity Falls (2012-2016) and director of the DuckTales (2017-2021) reboot. Gravity Falls' creator, Alex Hirsch also voices some characters in The Owl House. In an interview with CBR, Terrace explains the inspiration behind the series:

I rediscovered the works of Hieronymus Bosch, Remedios Varo, John Bauer and all these painters from between the 1500s to the 1800s that I just really admired and loved. I love medieval illuminations, and the little monsters in the corners. And I basically just thought, "I'd be cool if there was a cartoon like this." And I started doodling.
- Dana Terrace CBR Interview

Why Did The Owl House Get Cancelled By Disney?

Even though the series was well received by audiences and quickly grew a large fan-base, Disney cancelled the series after season 2. Many fans were upset by this cancellation and speculated that is was because the series has prominent LGBTQ+ representation as The Owl House Introduces Disney's First Non-Binary Character. In October 2021, Dana Terrace posted on Reddit:

... there are a few business people who oversee what fits into the Disney brand and one day one of those guys decided TOH didn't fit that "brand".

The series was able to conclude its storyline, however the third and final season was cut short. Instead of having the regular 20 episode season, similar to the first two seasons, season 3 was reduced to three specials, each 44 minutes long.
